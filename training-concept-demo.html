A unified approach to risk management training and tool adoption.

The Problem

It's not that people are untrained. It's that the system rewards the wrong behaviours.

Low Maturity (~1/3)

Risk applied inconsistently. Reactive instead of embedded. Same issues reoccur — learning loops are broken.

Fragmented & Siloed

Multiple disconnected processes. Inconsistent taxonomy. No end-to-end ownership. Tools mirror the fragmentation.

No Visibility

No single source of truth. Leadership reports take days of manual prep. Risk register creates false confidence.

The insight

Training people on the current broken process just produces well-trained people doing the wrong thing efficiently. The training must be inseparable from the new process and the new tool.

Layer 1 Exercise Demo

"Spot the Difference"

Participants compare two risk descriptions. Which one can you act on?

A
Traditional

"There is a risk that our systems could be hacked."

Likelihood: Medium
Impact: High
Controls: Firewall
B
Proactive

"Contractor VPN credentials are not revoked upon contract termination. 14 contractor accounts from the last 12 months remain active with access to the staging environment, which mirrors production data. No automated offboarding process exists."

Likelihood: Likely — credential reuse incidents occur ~quarterly; 3 confirmed active stale accounts with privileged access
Impact: Very High — staging contains production customer data; GDPR notification required
Trigger: Next vendor transition (Q3) adds more stale credentials

Discussion prompt

Which description helps a decision-maker act? Which one could be automated for monitoring? Which would you trust in a board report? — Everyone writes their answer first, then we discuss.

What is Proactive Risk Management?

Proactive risk management starts with understanding your business objectives, then identifies where uncertainty exists around those objectives. It's the difference between asking "what can go wrong?" (reactive) and "what are we uncertain about, and what does that mean for our goals?" (proactive).

The key shift in thinking

Instead of

"What bad things might happen?"

Think

"What are we trying to achieve, and what makes that uncertain?"

Interactive: The Proactive Risk Lifecycle

Business Context

Understand goals and objectives. Define areas of uncertainty. This is the foundation — a risk without business context is just a worry.

Good example

"Our objective is to complete the vendor migration by Q3. The uncertainty is around data continuity during cutover."

Weak example

"Something might go wrong with the migration."
